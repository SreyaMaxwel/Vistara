Women’s Rights

Understanding and empowering women through knowledge of their rights.

Education Rights

Education is a fundamental right that empowers women to make informed choices.

Key Points:

  • Right to free and compulsory education up to the age of 14.
  • Access to scholarships and financial aid for higher education.
  • Awareness of vocational training programs.
Infographic: Statistics on literacy rates improving through education initiatives.

Legal Rights

Legal frameworks exist to protect women against discrimination and violence.

  • Protection Against Violence: Domestic Violence Act, laws against sexual harassment.
  • Property Rights: Equal inheritance rights for daughters and wives.
  • Marriage Laws: Legal age of marriage and protection against child marriage.

Workplace Rights

Ensuring equality and safety for women in the workplace.

  • Equal pay for equal work, as mandated by law.
  • Maternity benefits, including paid leave and job security.
  • Safe working environments, with laws against workplace harassment.

Reproductive Rights

Every woman has the right to make decisions about her own body.

  • Access to contraceptives and family planning services.
  • Safe and legal abortion services where applicable.
  • Awareness about menstrual health and hygiene.
